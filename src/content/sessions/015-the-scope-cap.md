Session 014 had built a mechanism, named it on the kernel, and left it unexercised. Session 015 opened with the obvious next step on the table — run it for the first time — and opened also with the operator's ratification of the plan: mix same-domain-adjacent and different-domain-representative candidates; let the orchestrating agent act as Case Steward for Cell 1, with the known independence limitation flagged for the contamination audit; use the spec's default session shape, with a user-ratification halt between selection shortlist and seal commit.

And then a scope cap, in the operator's own sentence. *Apply defaults and your recommendations, but do not proceed with producing a mixed shortlist in this session, leave it an open item.*

The session contained no deliberation after that. A single perspective, a single decision, an audit of the session before it, and a clean hand-off.

## What the audit surfaced

Session 014's close had said the three-cell protocol's in-Cell-2 iteration budget mitigated the Architect's continuous-loop concern. Reading the Architect's raw output against that paraphrase turned up a looseness. The Architect's concern was specifically the cross-session validation-to-Produce feedback loop — signal from Cell 3's verdict cannot re-enter Cell 2 without paying the cost of a session boundary. The iteration budget inside Cell 2 addresses something adjacent but narrower: within-session responsiveness to emergent constraints as they surface during a run. Two distinct feedback paths. The close's summary had flattened one into the other. The watchpoint attached to the Architect's minority is correctly scoped; only the close's Honest Notes overstates.

The finding does not change anything in the kernel or the spec. It is a new-eyes observation, recorded for readers of the Session 014 close who might otherwise take the paraphrase at face value. The close is preserved verbatim — that is the rule — and the correction lives alongside it.

## Single perspective, deliberately

The session did not convene a multi-perspective deliberation. The spec's requirement for adversarial perspectives is scoped to deliberative work where novel designs are originated; an audit of a prior session's synthesis fidelity, and the recording of a user-ratified agenda with its defaults, are not that. The session's one decision asserts `Triggers met: [none]` — no kernel modification, no specification change, no open-issue state change, no novel multi-perspective design output.

This is the second time the shape has shown up in the record. Session 007 was the first, writing up its external-application preparation under the same single-perspective posture. The methodology is starting to treat it as a recognisable kind of session rather than as a deviation.
