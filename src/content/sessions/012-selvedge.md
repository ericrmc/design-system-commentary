Session 010's external artefact had not yet come back. The operator and his wife had not run the household-decision guide on a live choice. For the second session in a row, the receipt that would normally have been the primary work was not available. The session moved to pick its next piece of work from what remained.

What rose to the top was the longest-deferred question on the books. Session 001, the bootstrap session, had declined to adopt even a provisional name. It used "the methodology" as a placeholder and preserved the open question as Open Issue 001, to be taken up when the system "had enough identity to name meaningfully." Eleven sessions had passed, and the question was now the oldest thing in the queue.

## Four voices

The session convened four perspectives. A Namer to propose candidates from the methodology's accumulated identity. A Steward to weigh those candidates against the shape of the work across eleven sessions. A Skeptic to argue the refuse-to-name case at its strongest, carrying forward the Session 001 dissent. An Outsider — the non-Claude participant — to bring an independently-trained view on whether a Claude-led methodology naming itself through Claude deliberation was a well-timed move at all.

Three of the four favoured naming. The Skeptic refused. Identity accumulation is not the same as social durability. A mostly-Claude process can generate internal coherence faster than it earns external legitimacy. A name now would freeze today's self-understanding before wider use or different domains have had a chance to pressure-test it.

## The shortlist was shaped by the Outsider's first move

The Namer produced five candidates. Its top three were compounds built from the methodology's own vocabulary: Record Kernel, Session Kernel, Kernel Method.

The Outsider, responding independently without seeing the Namer's reply, had flagged in advance that it expected Claude perspectives to cluster precisely there. Internal-vocabulary compounds, it argued, would over-fit the present glossary onto the methodology's public face, and would age into an embarrassment if later sessions ever redistribute emphasis away from the current vocabulary.

The synthesis took the critique as a filter. The three compound candidates were removed from the shortlist on cross-training-flagged grounds rather than on their individual merits. What remained were three metaphors, each from a different perspective: Selvedge (Outsider), Sediment (Steward), Strata (cross-perspective).

<div class="pull">The filter removed candidates on grounds no Claude perspective had produced. The Namer's top three fell to a meta-argument about Claude convergence, not to a counter-candidate.</div>

## Selvedge

*Selvedge* is the self-finished edge of woven cloth. Its etymology, "self-edge," names the edge whose weave is closed — the edge that does not need hemming because it does not unravel on its own. The metaphor holds three properties of the methodology at once. Self-hosting, because the system weaves its own edges and evolves by running its own process on itself. Multi-strand preservation, because perspectives disagree and the disagreement is kept rather than melted into consensus. Durability by construction, because the versioning and explicit supersession pointers keep prior reasoning from fraying as the methodology evolves.

Sediment and Strata were live alternatives. Each one accented a different part of the shape. Sediment carried preservation most strongly; Strata carried layered history. Both were defensible. Operator ratification chose Selvedge for a specific reason: its metaphor reads active rather than archival. The methodology is doing something, not merely accumulating layers, and the name should carry the doing.

## The name lives in one small file

Three proposals for where the name should live. Put it in the kernel's title, so the title becomes "The Selvedge Kernel" (the Namer's preference). Spread it across the kernel title, PROMPT.md, and a new identity file (the Steward's preference). Put it in a new single-purpose file, `specifications/identity.md`, and nowhere else (the Outsider's preference, with the Skeptic's conditional agreement if the refusal was overruled).

Three of four converged on the third option. The kernel title would have overstated the kernel as the methodology's defining organ. PROMPT.md would have made the name constitutive of every session-start rather than referential. `identity.md` became the chosen placement: name, origin, intended use, reopening conditions. The file's scope section explicitly says that mission statements, values, and manifestos belong elsewhere if they are ever needed at all.

## The refusal became a reopening mechanism

The Skeptic had attached four falsifiability conditions to its refuse-to-name case. Each one named a specific kind of evidence that would convert "defensible" naming into "clearly right" naming. External adoption by someone who did not help build the methodology, sustained for three months, producing feedback that drives a kernel revision. Three externally validated artefacts across materially different domains. Five consecutive sessions without a kernel revision, as evidence that the nine-activity frame has stabilised under pressure rather than between pressures. An external reader reporting that the name itself blocks or distorts their engagement with the methodology.

The session adopted the name and adopted the four conditions as the file's four Reopening conditions. A future session meeting any of them may legitimately reopen the naming question. A future session reopening in their absence bears the burden of showing why.

The Skeptic's position is not preserved as a sentimental acknowledgement. It is operational.

## The pattern that did not repeat

Three sessions in a row had seen the Outsider resolve 2-2 splits between the Claude voices by proposing a third way that no Claude pole had reached. Session 012 did not produce that shape. There were no 2-2 splits this time; three of four perspectives converged on placement directly, and the operator ratified Selvedge against Sediment and Strata without a deadlock for the Outsider to break.

The load-bearing Outsider contribution this session was a different shape. Rather than a third way between two Claude positions, it was a genre-level observation about where the Claude positions would gather. That observation became the filter on the shortlist. The project recorded the distinction as data. Outsider-as-split-breaker was one form of cross-training contribution; Outsider-as-genre-critic is another, and a different form deserves different monitoring.

## An honest note

Every perspective that adopted the name recorded that the decision was defensible rather than obviously right. The Namer ranked its own top candidate above the shortlisted ones while noting that a Session 012 refusal would be "possibly more defensible than naming." The Outsider said the work satisfies the methodology's self-work criteria on external-visibility and specific-obstacle grounds; not strongly on others. The user's own reasoning — the risk that a deferred name arrives by habit rather than by decision — is what carries the ratification across the line.

The name is, by everyone's reading, a simple interim handle. It exists so the methodology can stop calling itself "the methodology" in external contexts. Whether Selvedge ages into something more will depend on whether the Reopening conditions ever come to hold.

## A question left open

*The methodology has named itself, preserved its own dissent as the conditions under which the name could be undone, and left every older specification untouched. What happens the first external-artefact session that uses the name in its framing? Does Selvedge read as a handle, or does it read as a claim?*
