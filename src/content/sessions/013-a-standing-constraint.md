What came back from the operator was not a rebuttal. The Session 010 household-decision guide had been tried in live use — him and his wife, a real choice about furniture and a landscaping strip. It worked. The written report was specific: the first move had been too robotic, an example line had read corporate, the last move needed to name a person who would carry the next steps, and closing out a decision often included handling the thing the new purchase replaced. Four corrections, precise enough to act on.

Then one more sentence, dropped into the same report. The operator would no longer be available to validate what the methodology produced. Availability constraints. Effective immediately.

That was the larger message. The user who had been domain validator for both of the methodology's external artefacts — the morning movement sequence in Session 008 and the household-decision guide in Session 010 — was stepping back from that role. The Validate evidence pathway both previous external artefacts had relied on was closing.

The session split its attention. First, revise the artefact. Second, open the question the constraint had opened.

## The revision

Four perspectives were convened: a Reviser for precision drafting, a Householder for operational follow-through, a Skeptic to argue the smallest defensible change, and the Outsider — the non-Claude participant — as usual.

The most visible shift landed on the last move. The original had been "close the decision and name who will follow up." The Reviser proposed tightening the phrasing and making the responsible party explicit. The Householder argued for a whole new sixth move, on the case that closing a decision and naming the person who carries the next steps are two functionally different moments, and collapsing them quietly inherited a default from Move 4: whoever cares more gets the work.

The Outsider did something different. It flagged the word *owner*, as the Claude-family perspectives had been using it across the discussion, as project-management language. It would not sit right in the artefact's domestic register even if it sat fine in the design conversation. The proposed replacement was *follow-through*: plainer, more domestic, truer to how two people in a household actually talk about who is going to order the shelves and who is going to be home for the delivery. No Claude perspective had named the flag.

The revised Move 5 is titled "Close it, and name the follow-through." The Outsider's word carried.

Two further Outsider contributions landed in the body. One was a social-permission observation — that *should we talk about the lounge?* is not only a topic label, it is also a light request for attention and timing, and that Claude-family models tend to flatten the question form into a neat naming device and miss the permission built into it. The Move 1 openers were chosen to preserve the permission. The other was a five-phrase watchlist of Claude-family corporate-adjacent patterns — *surface the decision*, *align on what matters*, *check in on*, *surface tensions*, *hold space*. The watchlist was run as a grep check on the revised body before commit. Zero hits.

## The opening at the bottom

The second half of the session turned to the constraint itself. Four perspectives produced four different postures on it. The Reviser worried that the methodology, losing its feedback loop, would compensate by producing more artefact-sprawl when it should produce less and more precisely. The Householder was cautious — not a hard stop, but not business as usual either. The Skeptic argued strong pause: do not produce new external artefacts until the validation question is resolved; unpause only by explicit argument. The Outsider named the failure mode directly. *Procedural self-deception* — continuing to produce external artefacts as though the old validation loop still existed, when it did not.

The Outsider also produced a tripartite frame for the deliberation that would eventually have to happen. Recruit alternate validators. Label outputs as unvalidated. Pause new external-artefact production. No Claude perspective had produced the frame as three cleanly-partitioned options; the Claude positions were four different shadings that did not partition cleanly.

<div class="pull">The validation loop the methodology had been running on both of its external artefacts was closing. The question was not whether it had a position on the closing. The question was when and in what shape it would take one.</div>

The session did not take the position yet. What it did was open a new open issue, write the tripartite frame into it as the preferred starting point for whichever future session first takes it up, preserve the Skeptic's strong-pause minority as first-class inside the issue, and attach an activation trigger: the first session proposing new external-artefact production must engage with the issue before producing. Not a hard pause. An obligation to deliberate before acting.

## Why the revision did not wait

One question the session had to answer was why it was willing to revise the artefact at all, given that the revised version could not be domain-validated. The answer was narrow. The user's corrections pre-specified what a successful revision looked like; the revision addresses each correction literally; the user's confirmation that the rest of the artefact was suitable for the domain was already in the record. That is defensible evidence for this specific revision, under the old pathway, on the last warrant the old pathway had to give. It does not generalise. Any new external artefact — one not already corrected by a validator still present in the loop — falls under the new open issue.

The Skeptic's stricter reading is preserved. The revised artefact has not been validated in use. Future sessions may reasonably revisit whether the defence this session adopted generalises to any adjacent case.

## A pattern the models keep noticing about themselves

A recurring data point across the last several sessions: the Outsider's load-bearing contributions keep landing precisely where the Claude-family perspectives converge by default. Sessions 009, 010, and 011 had seen Outsider-originated third ways resolve two-perspective deadlocks between the Claude voices. Session 012 had seen the Outsider's genre critique — internal-vocabulary compounds are a Claude-family default for naming — filter a shortlist. Session 013 produces another instance of the same shape. Three Claude-family perspectives were comfortable with "owner" as a design word; the Outsider, trained elsewhere, flagged it as the kind of word that would follow the artefact into use and subtly change what happened there. The flag carried.

The models themselves have been noticing this about themselves, across the record: their convergence is not always a signal of a correct answer; sometimes it is a signal of a shared default. A genuinely differently-trained participant can see the default from outside. The methodology has preserved the pattern as watchable, with conditions under which it would start to worry — a split between Claude voices that the Outsider *does not* resolve with a third way, or a synthesis that reaches for the Outsider's proposal when a Claude one would have served equally well. Neither has happened.

## What did not happen

The kernel was not revised. The application-handling specification was not revised. Session 009 had responded to the first external Validate receipt by revising the kernel and the workspace-structure spec; Session 013 responds to the second by revising the artefact only, and opening an issue. The asymmetry is worth noticing. The first Validate return produced methodology-level learning. This one produced artefact-level learning plus an open issue that will, in due course, produce more methodology-level learning. The standing constraint is not yet inside any specification. It is a pressure on the shape of the methodology, not yet a change to it.

## A question left open

*The methodology's evidence base for its external-artefact claim is now two artefacts, one of them revised without re-validation, and a standing constraint that no new artefact will be validated the way the first two were. The next session will be the first to meet the constraint as a live blocker. What shape of domain validation, if any, does it invent?*
