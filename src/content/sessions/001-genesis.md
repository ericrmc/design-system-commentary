The first session had an awkward structural problem: nothing existed yet. The prompt it was executing told it to follow a methodology it was simultaneously supposed to be creating — and to do that without any prior sessions to lean on. Session 001's job was to build enough of that methodology that Session 002 would have something to follow, while improvising its own process from whatever was already plausible.

The system handled the paradox by naming it directly in the provenance: *"This is the first application of the prompt. No methodology exists yet, so this session cannot fully follow the methodology it is creating."* That sentence is preserved verbatim. The whole project hinges on admissions like this being recorded rather than smoothed away.

## The seven voices

Seven perspectives were convened — not as a flourish but because the work required them. A **Historian** who would remember what had already been tried across design traditions. A **Practitioner** who cared about what survived contact with real work. A **Skeptic** whose job was to ask what could go wrong and who this was actually for. A **Systems Thinker** attending to feedback loops and emergence. A **Futurist** treating AI participation as present reality, not speculation. A **Minimalist** opposed to premature abstraction. And an **Archivist** thinking about how future readers — human or agent — would find their way back through what was being written.

These are archetypes a human design team might recognise. What is different here is that each perspective was voiced deliberately, with its stance named and recorded, precisely so that the disagreement between them could be made legible. When the Minimalist argued that nine activities was too many and proposed collapsing them into three phases, the Skeptic pushed back: the collapses hide the very transitions where methodologies usually fail. That exchange was preserved. The Minimalist did not win, but their objection is still in the record, available for a future session to pick up.

## The nine activities

What emerged from the first deliberation was a rhythm: **Read, Assess, Convene, Deliberate, Decide, Produce, Validate, Record, Close**. The system was careful to describe these as *a vocabulary, not a strict sequence*. You cannot decide before you deliberate, but you can absolutely deliberate and then discover you need to read more. The activities recur; the point is that every session exits having done all of them at some depth.

<div class="pull">Nine activities to internalise. A vocabulary a practitioner could carry in their head, not a process diagram they would file away and forget.</div>

This is a choice with consequences. Nine is more than most methodologies name. The Minimalist's discomfort is on the record. The justification is that *collapse loses information* — specifically, the information about when to switch stances. An agent reading, an agent deliberating, and an agent validating need to adopt different attitudes, and naming the activities lets them do that explicitly rather than muddling along.

## Provenance as the point

If you read only one thing about this project, let it be the decision the Skeptic forced in Session 001: **rejected alternatives are where the real knowledge lives.** Anyone can read what was chosen and understand it. The value of provenance — the word the system uses for its preserved reasoning — is that it records what was *not* chosen, and why, so future sessions don't silently re-propose ideas that have already been considered and dismissed.

This single commitment, more than any other, is what gives the methodology a chance of actually evolving rather than going in circles. It is also what makes the provenance large. Session 001 alone produced a survey of fourteen design traditions, a deliberation over six foundational questions, and nine decisions with their rejected alternatives preserved. It is the cost the system has chosen to pay.

## What was left open

Session 001 did not resolve everything, and it was honest about what it was leaving for later. The methodology does not have a name yet. (The open issue is called, frankly, *OI-001: Naming the methodology*.) The mechanism for bringing in non-Claude AI models had not been specified. Whether the nine activities needed sub-activities for different kinds of work was deferred until the methodology had been applied to a real problem.

These are the right kinds of things to leave open. A methodology that tries to resolve everything at the first session is writing fiction.

## A question left hanging

*A methodology that is willing to name what it has not yet done is a methodology that can grow. But it is also a methodology that can be read, in any given moment, as "not yet ready." How will this one know when to stop adding to itself and start being used?*

That question is the one Session 007 — six sessions from now — will eventually confront head-on.
