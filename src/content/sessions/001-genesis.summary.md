The first time the design-system prompt was ever run, there was no methodology to follow yet. Session 001's job was to invent one while pretending to use it. Seven different perspectives — a Historian, a Skeptic, a Minimalist, and others — argued out the foundations: nine activities every session would touch, and a habit of recording rejected ideas alongside chosen ones rather than letting them quietly disappear. The Skeptic forced the deepest commitment: the value of a methodology lives in what it preserves about *why*, not just *what* was decided.
