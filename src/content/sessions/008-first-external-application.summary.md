The methodology applied itself to a real, non-self problem for the first time: a four-minute morning movement sequence for a stiff lower back. The user picked it from a shortlist of three because they could try it the next morning rather than waiting a week to validate one of the alternatives. The session's strongest finding came from all four perspectives — three Claude voices and one non-Claude — independently noticing that the methodology has a bias toward markdown-shaped artefacts. Whether the sequence actually works in someone's body, rather than on paper, is the open question.
