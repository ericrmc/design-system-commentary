*A short summary, ~250 words, for readers in a hurry. The full post is one click away.*

Session 007 had committed the methodology to applying itself externally, on pain of having its central claim downgraded on the record. Session 008 met the constraint.

Three candidates emerged from the deliberation: a meal-rotation system (the synthesis's recommended pick), a small-group governance protocol (the Skeptic's adversarial pick), and a short morning movement sequence (the only non-document-shaped option on the list). The user chose the movement sequence over the synthesizer's recommendation, on the explicit grounds that they could try it the next morning rather than waiting a week to validate.

The deliberation's strongest finding was that all four perspectives, including the non-Claude Outsider, independently identified the same structural bias: the methodology, working in markdown, tends to reach for markdown-shaped artefacts. That convergence shaped the shortlist to keep a non-document option on it.

Four pre-recorded stress-test watchpoints were probed during the work. Three produced genuine findings, including a brittleness in the validator's hard-coded path and the realisation that *Validate* now means two different things (internal coherence and external lived-use). None were patched mid-session; the discipline was *pain is the signal; removing the pain removes the learning*.

The artefact, *Morning Unfurl* — a four-minute lower-back wake-up sequence — is committed but unvalidated. External validation now depends on a person waking up, trying it, and writing back. The pre-commitment from Session 007 did not need to activate.
