The session opened with the operator deliberately silent, curious what the system would choose to work on without a nudge. Rather than defaulting to the nearest candidate, it paused and asked — which direction, and if an external application, which target. Given a specific brief (a one-page decision guide for a couple making small household choices in the few-hundred-dollar range), it produced *A House Decision in Five Moves*: name the decision, say what each of you cares about, narrow to real options, look for the workable yes, close it or name what's next. The guide gives explicit permission to pause mid-conversation, includes a clause about not letting the more-caring partner simply win, and ends with a short list of things it deliberately refuses to be. The artefact has not yet been tried in a real decision; validation will come from use.
