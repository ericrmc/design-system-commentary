Session 007 left the methodology under a binding constraint: apply itself to something external, or have its central claim downgraded on the record. Session 008 had to act. It did.

What it produced, on the other side of that action, is a short morning movement sequence for a stiff lower back. Eight moves, supine to child's pose, written in the vocabulary of breath and articulation rather than activities and decisions. A reader with no methodology context can extract the file and follow it. That extractability is the test the previous seven sessions had been preparing for.

## The shortlist

Three candidates came out of the deliberation. A meal-rotation system, which was the synthesis's recommended pick and also the candidate the non-Claude Outsider independently arrived at. A small-group governance protocol, which the Skeptic put forward as an adversarial choice: the option they themselves would not have preferred but were holding open for the deliberation. And a short morning movement sequence, which the Explorer proposed and which carried one quietly important property: it was the only candidate on the list that was not document-shaped.

A fourth proposed candidate, a training plan, was demoted from the shortlist before the user ever saw it. It looked too much like the others. Three documents and a fourth document was not enough range to test what the methodology actually does outside itself.

## The choice that wasn't the recommendation

The synthesis recommended the meal-rotation. The user picked the movement sequence.

The reasoning was specific. *"The fact I'd actually do it will help to ensure we can Validate,"* the user wrote, in the message ratifying Candidate 3. Meal-rotation would have required a week of cooking-nights to evaluate. Movement could be tried tomorrow morning and reported back on the same day.

This is a substantively different judgment from the synthesis's. The synthesizer was weighing the eight criteria the deliberation had agreed on. The user was weighing whether external validation would actually happen on a timeline that did not stall the next session. Both judgments are legitimate. The structure of the session, delegated selection with user ratification, exists precisely so the second kind of judgment can override the first without anyone pretending it did not happen. The synthesizer's recommendation is preserved. The user's choice is preserved. The gap between them is preserved as part of the record.

## The four-way finding

The single most interesting thing in Session 008's deliberation was not any individual perspective's candidate. It was a structural observation that all four perspectives, three Claude variants and the non-Claude Outsider, independently arrived at, each in their own context, on their own.

The observation has several names in the raw outputs: *representability bias*, *document-shape bias*, *artefact-shape bias*, *medium bias*. The phrasings differ. The substance converges. Each perspective noticed that the candidates they were reaching for tended to be things that could be written down in prose. A methodology whose entire workspace is markdown files will tend, when asked to design something, to propose things that can be served back as more markdown.

The convergence crossed the model-family axis. The Outsider, working from different training data and a different vocabulary, arrived at the same structural claim. That is the strongest single cross-model signal across the four sessions in which a non-Claude voice has been in the room. It is also what shaped the final shortlist: the movement sequence was kept on the list precisely because it was the only option that was *not* document-shaped. Without that finding, the user would not have had the option to pick it.

## What broke gently

Session 007 had pre-recorded four watchpoints: places where the specifications were expected to strain when applied to an external domain. Session 008 probed them in the actual work and reported back honestly.

The Read activity, which the kernel describes as absorbing *"the full current state of the workspace,"* did not quite cover what was needed. Designing a movement sequence required absorbing the user's stated constraints *and* drawing on the agent's working knowledge of how a stiff lower back behaves first thing in the morning. The kernel names only the first kind of reading.

The Validate activity has quietly split into two senses. An internal one (does the validator pass; do the specifications still cohere) which happens in-session, and an external one (does the sequence actually work as movement design, when a person tries it on a soft mat) which cannot. The kernel uses the word for one sense; the work now requires both.

The workspace had no obvious place to put an external artefact, and the resolution surfaced something nobody had anticipated. The session's validator hard-codes the path to the decisions file. Putting the artefact at the natural numbered position would have shifted the decisions file by one and silently bypassed the validator's most recent checks. The artefact was given a non-numbered filename to keep the validator honest. The brittleness is recorded as a finding rather than fixed as a patch.

The fourth watchpoint, on self-referential phrasing in the specifications, barely bit. Worth noting; not worth fixing yet.

## What is still pending

Session 008 closes with the artefact committed and unvalidated. External validation in this domain is not something the methodology can perform on its own. It requires a person waking up, trying the sequence, noticing whether their low back feels different, and writing back. That is what external Validate looks like here, and it has to happen outside any session boundary.

The pre-commitment from Session 007, the methodology-claim downgrade provision, did not activate. The commitment did its job by shaping the constraint, not by punishing a failure. This is what such mechanisms are supposed to do when they work, and it is also part of why they are easy to underestimate from the outside.

## A question left open

*The methodology has now produced something that must be judged by a body, not by a validator. What does it mean for a self-hosting methodology to start depending, for the first time, on something it cannot itself check?*
